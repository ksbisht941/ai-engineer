Gaussian (Normal) Distribution – Quick Notes

Definition:
A Gaussian (Normal) Distribution is a **symmetric, bell-shaped probability distribution** where most values cluster around the mean.

---

Mathematical Expression:
f(x) = (1 / (σ√(2π))) * e^{-(x − μ)² / (2σ²)}

Where:

* μ → mean (center)
* σ → standard deviation (spread)

---

Key Properties:

* Symmetrical around mean
* Mean = Median = Mode
* Bell-shaped curve
* Defined by μ (location) and σ (spread)

---

Empirical Rule (68–95–99.7 Rule):

* 68% data → within ±1σ
* 95% data → within ±2σ
* 99.7% data → within ±3σ

---

Why it matters:

* Many ML models assume normality
* Used in statistics, hypothesis testing, Z-score
* Helps in outlier detection

---

When to use:

* When data looks symmetric and bell-shaped
* When applying statistical methods (Z-test, regression assumptions)

---

Example:
Heights of people, exam scores → often follow normal distribution

---

Key points:

* Central limit theorem → many real-world data approximate normal
* Sensitive to outliers
* If data is skewed → consider transformation (log, etc.)

---

Shortcut:

* Bell-shaped
* Center = mean
* Spread = standard deviation
* Most data near center
