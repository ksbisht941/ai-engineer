MSE (Mean Squared Error)

- definition:
  Average of squared differences between actual and predicted values.
  MSE = (1/n) * Σ (y_true - y_pred)^2

- why to use:
  To heavily penalize large errors and make model sensitive to outliers.

- when to use:
  When large errors are very costly and should be minimized strongly.

- advantage:
  Strong penalty for large errors
  Smooth and differentiable (good for optimization)

- disadvantage:
  Sensitive to outliers
  Units are squared (harder to interpret)
