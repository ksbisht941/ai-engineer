Regression Metrics – Quick Notes (MAE, MSE, R²)

---

1. MAE (Mean Absolute Error)

Definition:
Average of absolute differences between actual and predicted values.

Mathematical Expression:
MAE = (1/n) Σ |yi − ŷi|

Why use it:

* Easy to understand (same unit as target)
* Treats all errors equally

When to use:

* When you want a **robust metric** (less sensitive to outliers)

Example:
y = [2, 4, 5], ŷ = [2, 3, 4]
Errors → [0, 1, 1]
MAE = (0 + 1 + 1)/3 = 0.67

Key points:

* Simple and interpretable
* Not heavily affected by large errors

---

2. MSE (Mean Squared Error)

Definition:
Average of squared differences between actual and predicted values.

Mathematical Expression:
MSE = (1/n) Σ (yi − ŷi)²

Why use it:

* Penalizes large errors more (squared)

When to use:

* When large errors are **very costly**

Example:
Errors → [0, 1, 1]
Squared → [0, 1, 1]
MSE = (0 + 1 + 1)/3 = 0.67

Key points:

* Sensitive to outliers
* Widely used in optimization

---

3. R² Score (Coefficient of Determination)

Definition:
Measures how well the model explains variance in the target variable.

Mathematical Expression:
R² = 1 − (RSS / TSS)

Where:

* RSS = Σ (yi − ŷi)²
* TSS = Σ (yi − ȳ)²

Why use it:

* Shows **goodness of fit** (how much variance explained)

When to use:

* To evaluate overall model performance

Example:
R² = 0.8 → model explains 80% of variance

Key points:

* Range: (-∞ to 1)
* 1 → perfect model
* 0 → no better than mean
* Negative → worse than mean

---

Shortcut:

* MAE → simple, robust
* MSE → punishes big errors
* R² → explains variance
