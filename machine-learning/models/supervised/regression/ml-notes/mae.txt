MAE (Mean Absolute Error)

- definition:
  Average of absolute differences between actual and predicted values.
  MAE = (1/n) * Σ |y_true - y_pred|

- why to use:
  To measure average magnitude of errors in a simple, interpretable way.

- when to use:
  When all errors should be treated equally (no extra penalty for large errors).

- advantage:
  Easy to understand (same units as target)
  Not sensitive to outliers

- disadvantage:
  Does not penalize large errors strongly
  Less useful when big mistakes are critical
